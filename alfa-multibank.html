Multibank Experience

Role

Product design, scenario design, information hierarchy, and cross-functional collaboration.

Platform

Digital banking flows spanning account aggregation and overview experiences.

Focus

Data visibility, confidence in financial information, and simpler connected-bank journeys.

Overview

Multibank scenarios tend to become confusing quickly: users need trust in the data, a clear picture of what is connected, and a simple mental model for what they can do next.

The work focused on shaping those moments into something more legible and less cognitively demanding, especially in dense financial contexts.

My role was to structure these journeys so users could better understand their external accounts, available actions, and the reliability of the information shown inside Alfa-Bank.

Designing Clearer Cross-Bank Money Overview Scenarios

Key screens

Description

Multibank experiences become difficult when too much raw financial data is exposed without a clear product narrative. Users need to understand what accounts are connected, what information can be trusted, and which action makes sense next.

The work focused on making cross-bank states easier to scan and less cognitively demanding, especially in moments where users compare balances, review account visibility, or return to a partially configured connection.

Design goal Help users feel oriented in cross-bank situations by turning fragmented financial data into understandable product states.
  • Structured account aggregation scenarios around clarity rather than raw data volume.
  • Defined presentation patterns for external account visibility and money overview states.
  • Reduced ambiguity in connected-bank steps and interaction logic.
  • Supported more comprehensible financial decision-making through simpler information design.

The resulting direction created a more cohesive multibank experience, improving how connected accounts, balances, and next-step actions are understood within the main banking product.
